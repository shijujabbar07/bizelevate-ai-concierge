# Sales Call Checklist

Use this before and during every prospect call.

---

## Before the Call (2 minutes)

- [ ] Open [ONE-PAGER.md](../gtm/ONE-PAGER.md) — have it visible
- [ ] Know which product you're leading with (usually CustomerReach Respond)
- [ ] Know the prospect's business type (clinic, physio, PT, etc.)
- [ ] Have a rough idea of their call volume if known

---

## Opening (Know Their Pain First)

Ask before pitching:

- "How many calls would you say you miss in a week?"
- "What happens when someone can't get through — do they leave a voicemail, or just move on?"
- "Is there someone whose job it is to call them back?"

Let them talk. The pain sells itself.

---

## The One-Line Pitch

> "We set up an automated system that texts anyone who calls and doesn't get through — with their name, a link to book, and a follow-up if they don't respond. Takes about a day to set up, and it runs without your staff touching it."

---

## Objection Scripts

### "We already use ChatGPT"

> "Using a tool and building a system are different. This runs 24/7 without staff touching it — nothing falls through when they're busy or off."

Follow-up if needed: "ChatGPT doesn't watch your phone line. This does."

---

### "It's too expensive right now"

> "What does one missed appointment cost you? You only need to recover one per month for this to pay for itself. At $199, that's one booking."

Follow-up if needed: "I can show you the numbers — most clinics we've spoken to miss 5 to 10 calls a week. Even recovering two of those covers the cost."

---

### "We need to think about it"

> "Totally fair. How many calls did you miss last week? That number keeps running while you think. When's the right time to stop?"

Follow-up if needed: "What would need to be true for this to be a clear yes?"

---

### "What if it breaks?"

> "The retainer covers that. You're not buying software — you're buying a managed service. We monitor and fix it. That's what you're paying for."

Follow-up if needed: "And if something does go wrong, you call me directly. That's the difference between a managed service and a SaaS tool."

---

## Closing Move

Don't ask "what do you think?" — ask:

> "Does it make sense to try this for 30 days and see what it recovers?"

If yes: take their Twilio number or arrange the setup call.
If not yet: book a follow-up with a specific date.

---

## Pricing to Quote

| Plan | Price | What to say |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Starter (Respond only) | $199/month | "Missed call SMS, runs itself" |
| Core (Respond + Answer) | $499/month | "Adds the AI voice concierge for after hours" |
| Setup fee | $1,500 minimum | "One-time. Covers build, testing, and go-live" |

Always quote setup + first month together: "$1,699 to start, then $199/month."
